# MTSA Cybersecurity Requirements: Complete Implementation Guide

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive guide documents **all cybersecurity requirements** under the Maritime Transportation Security Act (MTSA) as established by the U.S. Coast Guard's final rule on "Cybersecurity in the Marine Transportation System" (effective July 16, 2025). This guide provides detailed requirements, recommendations, implementation steps, and a complete timeline for chemical companies with multiple Coast Guard-regulated sites.

### ⚠️ Mandatory Coast Guard Deadlines (Regulatory Requirements)

**These dates are MANDATORY and must be met. Failure to meet these deadlines may result in non-compliance.**

1. **July 16, 2025** - **MANDATORY**: Maritime cybersecurity and reporting obligations under **33 CFR Part 101** Subpart F (and related reporting rules) take effect—use **NVIC 02-24, Change 1** with **33 CFR 101.305**, **101.620**, and **33 CFR Part 6 / 6.16-1** to determine **NRC**, **COTP**, **FBI**, and **CISA** notifications (not a single “NRC-only” path for all cyber events)
2. **January 12, 2026** - **MANDATORY**: Initial cybersecurity training for all IT/OT personnel must be completed
3. **July 16, 2027** - **MANDATORY**: 
   - Cybersecurity Officer (CySO) must be designated
   - Initial Cybersecurity Assessment must be completed
   - Cybersecurity Plan must be submitted to Coast Guard for approval

### 📋 Recommended Start Dates

The timeline in Section 12 includes **recommended start dates** for activities to ensure you meet the mandatory deadlines above. These recommendations are not regulatory requirements, but starting earlier provides buffer time and ensures readiness.

## 1. Regulatory Framework

### 1.1 Final Rule: "Cybersecurity in the Marine Transportation System"

**Effective Date**: July 16, 2025

**Authority**: 33 CFR Part 101 (MTSA Regulations)

**Purpose**: Enhance the security and resilience of the Marine Transportation System (MTS) against evolving cyber threats

### 1.2 Related Guidance

- **NVIC 01-20**: Guidelines for Addressing Cyber Risks at MTSA-Regulated Facilities
- **NVIC 02-24** and **NVIC 02-24, Change 1**: U.S. Coast Guard guidance on reporting breaches of security, suspicious activity, transportation security incidents, and cyber incidents (use the **current** NVIC PDF posted under the Coast Guard Maritime Cybersecurity resources)
- **CG-5PC Policy Letter 01-25**: U.S. Coast Guard policy on cybersecurity **training** implementation (aligns with **33 CFR 101.650(d)**)
- **Small Entity Compliance Guide**: Detailed guidance for facilities (published by Coast Guard)
- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework**: Recommended framework for cybersecurity measures (see NVIC 01-20)
- **Maritime Cybersecurity resource hub**: `https://www.uscg.mil/MaritimeCyber/` (fact sheets, guides, and pointers to official PDFs)
- **Regulatory questions**: **MTSCyberRule@uscg.mil** (contact published in U.S. Coast Guard announcements on the cyber final rule)

### 1.3 Scope of Application

Applies to all facilities regulated under MTSA, including:
- Chemical facilities with shipping operations
- Facilities handling Certain Dangerous Cargoes (CDCs)
- Facilities receiving vessels subject to SOLAS
- Facilities transferring cargo to/from vessels

## 2. Complete Requirements Overview

The MTSA cybersecurity requirements consist of **eight major requirement categories**:

1. **Cybersecurity Plan** - Comprehensive plan addressing preparation, prevention, and response
2. **Cybersecurity Officer (CySO)** - Designated officer responsible for cybersecurity
3. **Cybersecurity Training** - Training for all IT/OT personnel
4. **Cybersecurity Assessment** - Annual assessment of vulnerabilities and risks
5. **Cybersecurity Drills and Exercises** - Regular testing of cybersecurity capabilities
6. **Incident Reporting** - Security and cyber reporting per **33 CFR 101.305**, **101.620**, and **33 CFR 6.16-1**, as harmonized in **NVIC 02-24, Change 1** (NRC, COTP, FBI, CISA as applicable)
7. **Technical Cybersecurity Measures** - Implementation of **33 CFR 101.650** controls (account, device, data, risk management—including assessment and **penetration testing at plan renewal**, **KEV** remediation—supply chain, resilience, network segmentation, physical security, plus training in **(d)**)
8. **Recordkeeping** - Maintenance of cybersecurity records

## 3. Requirement 1: Cybersecurity Plan

### 3.1 Regulatory Requirement

Facilities must develop and maintain a **Cybersecurity Plan** that addresses:
- **Preparation**: Strategies for preparing for cybersecurity threats
- **Prevention**: Measures to prevent cybersecurity incidents
- **Response**: Procedures for responding to cybersecurity incidents
- **Recovery**: Capabilities for recovering from cybersecurity incidents

### 3.2 Submission Deadline

**July 16, 2027**: Cybersecurity Plan must be submitted to the U.S. Coast Guard for approval

### 3.3 Plan Requirements

#### 3.3.1 Plan Development

The Cybersecurity Plan must be:
- **Facility-specific**: Tailored to the facility's specific risks and operational context
- **Risk-based**: Based on findings from the Cybersecurity Assessment
- **Comprehensive**: Address all identified vulnerabilities and threats
- **Integrated**: Integrated with the existing Facility Security Plan (FSP)

#### 3.3.2 Plan Components

The Cybersecurity Plan must include:

1. **Risk Assessment Summary**
   - Identified vulnerabilities
   - Threat assessment
   - Risk prioritization

2. **Preventive Measures**
   - Technical controls
   - Administrative controls
   - Physical controls
   - Network segmentation strategies

3. **Detection Capabilities**
   - Monitoring systems
   - Alert mechanisms
   - Threat detection procedures

4. **Response Procedures**
   - Incident response plan
   - Escalation procedures
   - Communication protocols
   - Coordination with authorities

5. **Recovery Strategies**
   - Business continuity plans
   - System recovery procedures
   - Data backup and restoration
   - Operational restoration

6. **Integration with FSP**
   - How cybersecurity integrates with physical security
   - Coordination between cyber and physical security measures
   - Unified security approach

### 3.4 Implementation Recommendations

#### Step 1: Conduct Cybersecurity Assessment (See Requirement 4)
- Complete assessment before developing plan
- Use assessment findings to inform plan development

#### Step 2: Develop Plan Structure
- Use NIST Cybersecurity Framework as guidance
- Align with existing FSP structure
- Ensure comprehensive coverage

#### Step 3: Document Preventive Measures
- Document all technical controls
- Document administrative procedures
- Document network segmentation approach
- Reference critical asset inventory

#### Step 4: Develop Response Procedures
- Create incident response playbooks
- Define escalation procedures
- Establish communication protocols
- Coordinate with National Response Center procedures

#### Step 5: Plan Integration
- Integrate with existing FSP
- Ensure coordination between cyber and physical security
- Document unified security approach

#### Step 6: Review and Approval
- Internal review by CySO and facility security team
- Legal/compliance review
- Submit to Coast Guard for approval by July 16, 2027

### 3.5 Best Practices

1. **Use NIST Framework**: Align plan with NIST Cybersecurity Framework
2. **Leverage Existing Plans**: Build on existing security plans and procedures
3. **Involve Stakeholders**: Include IT, OT, security, and operations teams
4. **Regular Updates**: Plan for annual updates based on assessment findings
5. **Testing**: Test plan through drills and exercises (see Requirement 5)

## 4. Requirement 2: Cybersecurity Officer (CySO)

### 4.1 Regulatory Requirement

Each facility must **designate a Cybersecurity Officer (CySO)** responsible for:
- Development of the Cybersecurity Plan
- Implementation of the Cybersecurity Plan
- Maintenance of the Cybersecurity Plan
- Serving as primary point of contact for cybersecurity matters

### 4.2 Designation Deadline

**July 16, 2027**: CySO must be designated

### 4.3 CySO Qualifications

**Regulatory baseline**: **33 CFR 101.625(e)** requires the CySO to have **general knowledge**, through training, education, or equivalent job experience, including at minimum:

1. General vessel, facility, or OCS facility operations and conditions  
2. General cybersecurity guidance and best practices  
3. The Cyber Incident Response Plan  
4. The Cybersecurity Plan  
5. Cybersecurity equipment and systems  
6. Methods of conducting cybersecurity audits, inspections, control, and monitoring techniques  
7. Relevant laws and regulations pertaining to cybersecurity  
8. Instruction techniques for cybersecurity training and education  
9. Handling of Sensitive Security Information and security-related communications  
10. Current cybersecurity threat patterns and **KEVs** (Known Exploited Vulnerabilities)  
11. Recognizing characteristics and behavioral patterns of persons who are likely to threaten security  
12. Conducting and assessing cybersecurity drills and exercises  

Facility programs should **map evidence** (résumés, training records, experience letters) to these elements for inspection readiness.

#### 4.3.1 Technical Knowledge (implementation emphasis)
- Understanding of IT and OT systems and how they support maritime operations
- Knowledge of cybersecurity threats, vulnerabilities, and **KEV** remediation expectations for **critical** systems

#### 4.3.2 Regulatory Knowledge (implementation emphasis)
- MTSA and **33 CFR Part 101** Subpart F requirements, including reporting and plan submission paths (e.g., cognizant COTP / OCMI)

#### 4.3.3 Operational Knowledge (implementation emphasis)
- Facility and shipping operations; integration of cyber measures with the **FSP** and **Cybersecurity Plan**

### 4.4 CySO Responsibilities

#### 4.4.1 Plan Development and Maintenance
- Lead development of Cybersecurity Plan
- Ensure plan addresses all requirements
- Coordinate plan updates based on assessments
- Maintain plan currency
- Ensure an **annual audit** of the Cybersecurity Plan and its implementation, and updates if necessary (**33 CFR 101.625(d)(3)**)

#### 4.4.2 Implementation Oversight
- Oversee implementation of technical measures
- Coordinate training programs
- Manage cybersecurity assessments
- Oversee drills and exercises

#### 4.4.3 Incident Management
- Serve as primary contact for cybersecurity incidents
- Coordinate incident response
- Manage incident reporting
- Coordinate with authorities
- Ensure **identification and mitigation of all KEVs** affecting **critical** IT or OT systems **without delay** (**33 CFR 101.625(d)(15)**; see also **101.650(e)(1)(iv)** and maintenance measures)

#### 4.4.4 Compliance Management
- Ensure compliance with MTSA requirements
- Coordinate with Coast Guard
- Manage recordkeeping
- Prepare for audits and inspections

### 4.5 Implementation Recommendations

#### Step 1: Identify Candidate
- Assess internal candidates
- Consider external candidates if needed
- Evaluate qualifications and experience

#### Step 2: Designate CySO
- Formally designate CySO
- Document designation
- Communicate to all relevant personnel

#### Step 3: Provide Training
- Ensure CySO has necessary training
- Provide MTSA-specific training
- Ensure familiarity with facility operations

#### Step 4: Establish Authority
- Define CySO authority and responsibilities
- Establish reporting structure
- Ensure access to necessary resources

#### Step 5: Document in FSP
- Include CySO designation in FSP
- Document responsibilities
- Update FSP as required

### 4.6 Best Practices

1. **Early Designation**: Designate CySO early to support implementation
2. **Adequate Authority**: Ensure CySO has authority to implement measures
3. **Resource Access**: Provide access to necessary resources and personnel
4. **Training**: Ensure ongoing training and professional development
5. **Backup**: Designate backup/alternate CySO for continuity

## 5. Requirement 3: Cybersecurity Training

### 5.1 Regulatory Requirement

All personnel with access to **Information Technology (IT)** and **Operational Technology (OT)** systems must complete cybersecurity training.

### 5.2 Training Deadlines

Per **33 CFR 101.650(d)(4)** and **CG-5PC Policy Letter 01-25** (Oct. 9, 2025; local copy: `documentation/reference-downloads/uscg-cyber-2025/Cybersecurity Training Policy Letter-5-PC_01-25.pdf`):

- **January 12, 2026**: All personnel must complete the training specified in **(d)(1)(ii) through (v)**; key personnel must complete **(d)(2)** by the same date  
- **Annually thereafter**: Same topics repeat on an annual basis (key personnel may need more frequent updates **as needed**)  
- **New personnel** (hired after the rule’s effective date): training within **5 days** of gaining system access, but **no later than 30 days from hiring**, then annually  
- **New IT or OT systems** (put in service after the effective date): training within **5 days** of system access, then annually  
- **Cybersecurity Plan content (d)(1)(i)**: within **60 days** of receiving **Coast Guard approval** of the Cybersecurity Plan **once a plan is approved**  
- **Interim before CSP approval / CySO assignment (Policy Letter §8.b)**: Training on **(d)(1)(i)** may be **deferred** until the **Cybersecurity Plan is approved** and a **CySO** is assigned, **but no later than July 16, 2027**. Training on **(d)(1)(iv)** (reporting to the CySO) may **temporarily** follow **existing cyber incident reporting procedures** under the **FSP/VSP/OCS FSP** until the CSP is in place. Trainers or approvers during this interim should meet or exceed **CySO knowledge** expectations in **33 CFR 101.625(e)**.

### 5.3 Training Requirements

#### 5.3.0 U.S. Coast Guard Policy Letter 01-25 (alignment)

Use the official policy for inspection readiness and program design (non-regulatory but describes Coast Guard verification expectations):

- **“Access” (Policy §7.a)**: Ability to interact with a system—including **logical** access **or** **unrestricted physical** access to spaces/areas housing **IT and/or OT** (e.g., physical presence that can reach equipment counts as access even without credentials).  
- **Key personnel (Policy §7.b)**: **Designated by the owner/operator**; typically includes roles involved in **cyber IR/recovery**, **cybersecurity oversight** of IT or **remotely accessible OT**, and other designated roles. The policy lists **examples** (CSO/FSO/VSO, admins, **CySO**, OT engineers with elevated access, etc.); **document** how your facility defines key personnel. **Key personnel** must receive **both** **101.650(d)(2)** topics **and** **(d)(1)** topics.  
- **Combination with existing security training (Policy §8)**: Cybersecurity training may be **combined** with training required under **33 CFR 104.225**, **105.215**, and **106.220** where appropriate.  
- **After CSP approval (Policy §8.c)**: The **CySO** must **review** existing training so it matches the entity’s **IT/OT** and operations.  
- **Untrained personnel (Policy §9; aligns with 101.650(d)(3))**: Use **physical** accompaniment/monitoring by trained personnel, or **remote escorting** (e.g., least-privilege remote access, session monitoring/recording, real-time controls) with procedures **documented**; **remote access to OT** by untrained personnel must include **remote control/shadowing** by a **trained** application engineer/owner as described in the policy.  
- **Training records (Policy §10)**: At minimum: **date**, **duration**, **outline** showing coverage of **101.650(d)(1)** and **(2)** topics, and **attendee list**; may align with FSP/VSP/OCS FSP security training records (plan amendment not always required when documented as additional security training—see policy).

#### 5.3.1 Scope of Training

Training must cover:
- **Cyber Threat Awareness**: Recognition of cyber threats
- **Vulnerability Identification**: Understanding of vulnerabilities
- **Incident Response**: Procedures for responding to incidents
- **Preventive Measures**: Best practices for preventing incidents
- **Reporting Requirements**: When and how to report incidents

#### 5.3.2 Training Audience

Training must be provided to:
- All personnel with **IT** system access (including **contractors**, all employment types)
- All personnel with **OT** system access (same scope)
- Personnel with **physical** access to areas housing IT/OT when that access is **unrestricted**, per Policy Letter **§7.a** (counts as “access” for training purposes)
- **Key personnel** as **defined and documented** by the owner/operator (**101.650(d)(2)** **plus** all **(d)(1)** topics)
- **CySO** and security personnel (CySO must meet **101.625(e)** knowledge areas; training should support that)

#### 5.3.3 Training Content

Training should include:
- **General Cyber Awareness**: Basic cybersecurity principles
- **Role-Specific Training**: Training tailored to job functions
- **Threat-Specific Training**: Training on specific threats relevant to operations
- **Procedural Training**: Training on facility-specific procedures

### 5.4 Implementation Recommendations

#### Step 1: Assess Training Needs
- Identify all personnel requiring training
- Assess current knowledge levels
- Identify role-specific training needs

#### Step 2: Develop Training Program
- Develop general awareness training
- Create role-specific training modules
- Develop facility-specific content
- Align with Cybersecurity Plan

#### Step 3: Deliver Training
- Schedule training sessions
- Deliver training to all required personnel
- Ensure completion by January 12, 2026
- Document completion

#### Step 4: Annual Training
- Schedule annual refresher training
- Update training content based on new threats
- Ensure all personnel complete annual training
- Document annual completion

#### Step 5: Evaluate Effectiveness
- Assess training effectiveness
- Gather feedback from participants
- Update training based on feedback
- Measure improvement in cybersecurity awareness

### 5.5 Best Practices

1. **Behavioral Science**: Incorporate behavioral science principles
2. **Role-Based**: Tailor training to specific roles and responsibilities
3. **Interactive**: Use interactive training methods
4. **Regular Updates**: Update training based on evolving threats
5. **Documentation**: Maintain comprehensive training records

## 6. Requirement 4: Cybersecurity Assessment

### 6.1 Regulatory Requirement

Facilities must conduct a **comprehensive Cybersecurity Assessment** to:
- Identify vulnerabilities
- Assess risks
- Evaluate effectiveness of existing security measures
- Inform Cybersecurity Plan development

### 6.2 Assessment Deadlines

- **July 16, 2027**: Initial assessment must be completed
- **Annually**: Assessments must be conducted annually thereafter

### 6.3 Assessment Requirements

#### 6.3.1 Assessment Scope

The assessment must cover:
- **Critical Assets**: Identification and assessment of critical assets
- **Vulnerabilities**: Identification of cybersecurity vulnerabilities
- **Threats**: Assessment of cyber threats
- **Risks**: Evaluation of risks to critical assets
- **Controls**: Evaluation of existing security controls
- **Gaps**: Identification of security gaps

#### 6.3.2 Assessment Methodology

The assessment should:
- Be comprehensive and systematic
- Use recognized frameworks (NIST, etc.)
- Include both IT and OT systems
- Consider interdependencies
- Assess both technical and administrative controls

#### 6.3.3 Assessment Components

The assessment must include:

1. **Asset Inventory**
   - Complete inventory of IT systems
   - Complete inventory of OT systems
   - Identification of critical assets
   - Documentation of system interdependencies

2. **Vulnerability Assessment**
   - Technical vulnerability scanning
   - Configuration review
   - Access control review
   - Network architecture review

3. **Threat Assessment**
   - Identification of potential threats
   - Assessment of threat likelihood
   - Evaluation of threat impact
   - Consideration of threat actors

4. **Risk Assessment**
   - Risk identification
   - Risk analysis
   - Risk prioritization
   - Risk treatment planning

5. **Control Evaluation**
   - Evaluation of existing controls
   - Identification of control gaps
   - Assessment of control effectiveness
   - Recommendations for improvement

### 6.4 Implementation Recommendations

#### Step 1: Prepare for Assessment
- Gather existing documentation
- Identify assessment team
- Define assessment scope
- Develop assessment plan

#### Step 2: Conduct Asset Inventory
- Inventory all IT systems
- Inventory all OT systems
- Identify critical assets (see Critical Assets Research)
- Document system interdependencies

#### Step 3: Conduct Vulnerability Assessment
- Perform technical vulnerability scanning
- Review system configurations
- Assess access controls
- Review network architecture

#### Step 4: Conduct Threat Assessment
- Identify potential threats
- Assess threat likelihood
- Evaluate threat impact
- Consider facility-specific threats

#### Step 5: Conduct Risk Assessment
- Analyze risks to critical assets
- Prioritize risks
- Develop risk treatment plans
- Document risk assessment

#### Step 6: Evaluate Controls
- Evaluate existing security controls
- Identify control gaps
- Assess control effectiveness
- Develop recommendations

#### Step 7: Document Assessment
- Document all findings
- Document vulnerabilities
- Document risks
- Document recommendations

#### Step 8: Use Assessment Results
- Use findings to develop Cybersecurity Plan
- Prioritize remediation efforts
- Update security measures
- Plan for annual reassessment

### 6.5 Best Practices

1. **Use Recognized Frameworks**: Align with NIST Cybersecurity Framework
2. **Comprehensive Coverage**: Ensure all systems are assessed
3. **Regular Assessments**: Conduct annual assessments
4. **Actionable Findings**: Ensure findings are actionable
5. **Continuous Improvement**: Use assessments for continuous improvement

## 8. Requirement 6: Incident Reporting

### 8.1 Regulatory Requirement

Reporting is **not** a single-channel “NRC only” rule. MTSA-regulated entities must apply:

- **33 CFR 101.305** — **Breach of security (BOS)** and **suspicious activity (SA)** that may result in a **TSI** (generally **NRC** without delay, per NVIC)  
- **33 CFR 101** TSI reporting — **local COTP** (facilities/vessels under **104/105**) or **District Commander** (**106**), then follow the **approved security plan** (which may include **NRC**)  
- **33 CFR 6.16-1** — **Cyber incidents** (Part 6 sense): notify **FBI**, **CISA**, and **Coast Guard (COTP)** as required by regulation  
- **33 CFR 101.620(b)(7)** / **101.650(g)(1)** — **Reportable cyber incidents (RCI)** to **NRC** when the entity **has not** reported under **6.16-1** or is **not subject** to it (e.g., **OCS** facilities under **106** per **NVIC 02-24 CH 1** discussion)

**Authoritative USCG harmonization:** **NVIC 02-24, Change 1** (Nov. 12, 2025; local copy: `documentation/reference-downloads/uscg-cyber-2025/BOS_SA_Cyber Reporting NVIC 02-24 CH 1.pdf`). When in doubt, the NVIC instructs entities to report under **101.305**, **101.620**, and **6.16-1** as applicable.

### 8.2 Reporting Deadline

**July 16, 2025**: Subpart F effective date; **cyber reporting** obligations must be read together with **Part 6** and **NVIC 02-24 CH 1**.

### 8.3 Reporting Requirements

#### 8.3.1 Cyber incident / RCI criteria (NVIC 02-24 CH 1, Enclosure (1))

The NVIC provides **non-exhaustive** criteria for cyber incidents / RCIs that must be reported (harmonizing **Part 6** “cyber incident” with **Part 101** “RCI”), including situations that **lead to** or **could reasonably lead to** (paraphrased for planning—cite the NVIC for exact wording):

- Substantial loss of **confidentiality, integrity, or availability** of covered **IT, network, or OT**  
- **Disruption** or significant adverse impact on **operations** or delivery of **goods/services**, including potential serious **health/safety** impacts  
- **Unauthorized** access or disclosure of **nonpublic personal information** for a **significant** number of individuals  
- Other potential **operational disruption** to **critical infrastructure** systems or assets  
- Incidents that may lead to a **TSI** under **33 CFR 101.105**  

**Not** every nuisance event is a “cyber incident”: routine **spam/phishing** handled by normal hygiene, and **accidental** minor policy violations, may fall below reporting thresholds—**NVIC** emphasizes **sophistication, volume, persistence**, and **TSI** potential. **Cyber-related** **BOS/SA** may still require **NRC** reporting when tied to security-plan systems or **TSI** potential.

#### 8.3.2 Where to report (NVIC 02-24 CH 1, Enclosure (1) §e)

| Event (examples) | Primary USCG-aligned channels |
|------------------|-------------------------------|
| **BOS** or **SA** (MTSA entity) | **NRC** **without delay**: **1-800-424-8802** (**33 CFR 101.305**). May also notify **COTP**; **does not** replace **NRC**. |
| **TSI** | **104/105**: **local COTP** without delay, then **security plan** procedures (may include **NRC**). **106**: **cognizant District Commander**, then plan procedures. |
| **Cyber incident** (**Part 6** / **6.16-1**) | **FBI**, **CISA**, and **COTP**. NVIC: **NRC** notification **without delay** is **strongly recommended** and **satisfies** the **COTP** notification; **NRC** forwards to **FBI CyWatch** (**cywatch@fbi.gov**), which the **FBI accepts** as meeting the **FBI** requirement. **Separate** notification to **CISA**: **(844) 729-2472**, **SayCISA@cisa.dhs.gov**, or CISA Service Portal. |
| **RCI** (**101.620**) | Entities **not subject** to **33 CFR Part 6** (e.g., many **OCS** cases per NVIC): **NRC** without delay. If the entity **already** reported the cyber incident under **6.16-1**, **101.620** reporting **does not** apply (avoid duplicate reports). |

Multi-zone COTP impact: NVIC instructs reporters to note in the **NRC** report if the incident may affect **more than one** COTP zone.

#### 8.3.3 Reporting timeline and content

- **Without delay / immediately** when criteria are met—do **not** wait for a full investigation to **initiate** notification.  
- Initial reports may be **minimal**; **SSI** handling applies (**49 CFR Part 1520**). NVIC: basic **who/where/what/type/summary** is sufficient initially; deeper vulnerability detail can follow with the Coast Guard.

## 9. Requirement 7: Technical Cybersecurity Measures

### 9.1 Regulatory Requirement

Facilities must implement the cybersecurity measures in **33 CFR 101.650**, including at minimum the categories addressed in **(a)** account security, **(b)** device security, **(c)** data security, **(d)** training (see Requirement 3), **(e)** risk management (including the **Cybersecurity Assessment** and related requirements), **(f)** supply chain, **(g)** resilience, **(h)** network segmentation, and **(i)** physical security measures for OT and related IT.

### 9.2 Account Security Measures

#### 9.2.1 Requirements

Implement measures to secure user accounts:
- **Strong Authentication**: Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- **Access Controls**: Role-based access controls (RBAC)
- **Account Management**: Regular review and management of accounts
- **Password Policies**: Strong password requirements
- **Privileged Access**: Special controls for privileged accounts

#### 9.2.2 Implementation Recommendations

1. **Implement MFA**: Require MFA for all critical system access
2. **Role-Based Access**: Implement RBAC aligned with job functions
3. **Regular Reviews**: Conduct regular access reviews
4. **Password Policies**: Enforce strong password policies
5. **Privileged Access Management**: Implement special controls for privileged accounts

### 9.3 Device Security Measures

#### 9.3.1 Requirements

Implement measures to secure devices:
- **Endpoint Protection**: Antivirus, anti-malware protection
- **Device Management**: Centralized device management
- **Patch Management**: Regular security updates and patches
- **Device Hardening**: Secure device configurations
- **Physical Security**: Physical protection of devices

#### 9.3.2 Implementation Recommendations

1. **Endpoint Protection**: Deploy comprehensive endpoint protection
2. **Patch Management**: Implement automated patch management
3. **Device Hardening**: Harden device configurations
4. **Inventory Management**: Maintain device inventory
5. **Physical Security**: Ensure physical security of devices

### 9.4 Data Security Measures

#### 9.4.1 Requirements

Implement measures to secure data:
- **Encryption**: Encrypt data at rest and in transit
- **Data Classification**: Classify data by sensitivity
- **Access Controls**: Control access to sensitive data
- **Backup and Recovery**: Regular backups and recovery testing
- **Data Loss Prevention**: Prevent unauthorized data access

#### 9.4.2 Implementation Recommendations

1. **Encryption**: Implement encryption for sensitive data
2. **Data Classification**: Classify all data
3. **Access Controls**: Implement data access controls
4. **Backup Strategy**: Implement comprehensive backup strategy
5. **Data Loss Prevention**: Deploy DLP solutions

### 9.5 Risk Management

#### 9.5.1 Requirements

Implement risk management processes per **33 CFR 101.650(e)**, including:

- **Cybersecurity Assessment**: Complete no later than **July 16, 2027**, and **at least annually thereafter**; conduct **sooner than annually** if there is a **change in ownership**; document outcomes in the **FSA/VSA/OCS FSA** as required (**104.305 / 105.305 / 106.305**); incorporate resolutions into the Cybersecurity Plan via amendment (**101.630(e)**)  
- **KEVs**: Patch or implement **documented compensating controls** for all **KEVs** in **critical** IT or OT systems **without delay**  
- **Penetration testing**: In conjunction with **Cybersecurity Plan renewal**, ensure a penetration test is completed; provide a **certification letter** and document **all identified vulnerabilities** in the **FSA/VSA/OCS FSA** per **33 CFR 104.305, 105.305, 106.305**  
- **Risk treatment and monitoring**: Ongoing treatment, monitoring, and reporting consistent with the approved Cybersecurity Plan

#### 9.5.2 Implementation Recommendations

1. **Risk Framework**: Implement risk management framework
2. **Regular Assessments**: Conduct regular risk assessments
3. **Risk Treatment**: Implement risk treatment measures
4. **Risk Monitoring**: Monitor risks continuously
5. **Risk Reporting**: Report risks to management

### 9.6 Resilience

#### 9.6.1 Requirements

Implement resilience measures:
- **Business Continuity**: Business continuity planning
- **Disaster Recovery**: Disaster recovery planning
- **Redundancy**: System redundancy
- **Failover Capabilities**: Automated failover
- **Recovery Testing**: Regular recovery testing

#### 9.6.2 Implementation Recommendations
