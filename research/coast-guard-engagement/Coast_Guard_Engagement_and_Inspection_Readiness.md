## 1. Understanding Coast Guard Structure

### 1.1 Coast Guard Districts

The U.S. Coast Guard is organized into **nine districts** covering different geographic regions:

- **District 1**: New England (Boston, MA)
- **District 5**: Mid-Atlantic (Portsmouth, VA)
- **District 7**: Southeast (Miami, FL)
- **District 8**: Gulf Coast (New Orleans, LA)
- **District 9**: Great Lakes (Cleveland, OH)
- **District 11**: Pacific Southwest (Alameda, CA)
- **District 13**: Pacific Northwest (Seattle, WA)
- **District 14**: Hawaii and Pacific (Honolulu, HI)
- **District 17**: Alaska (Juneau, AK)

### 1.2 Key Contacts

**District Office Responsibilities:**
- Review and approve Facility Security Plans (FSPs)
- Review and approve Cybersecurity Plans (by July 16, 2027)
- Conduct facility inspections
- Enforce MTSA regulations
- Provide guidance and clarification

**Finding Your District Office:**
- Identify your facility's geographic location
- Contact the appropriate District Office
- District contact information available on USCG website

### 1.3 MTSA Regulatory Authority

**Regulatory Basis:**
- **33 CFR Part 101**: MTSA Regulations
- **Final Rule**: "Cybersecurity in the Marine Transportation System" (effective July 16, 2025)
- **NVIC 01-20**: Guidelines for Addressing Cyber Risks at MTSA-Regulated Facilities

**Enforcement Authority:**
- Coast Guard has authority to inspect facilities
- Can issue violations and enforcement actions
- Can require corrective actions
- Can suspend or revoke facility approvals

## 2. Proactive Engagement with District Offices

### 2.1 Benefits of Proactive Engagement

**Early Engagement Advantages:**
- ‚úÖ Clarify requirements before implementation
- ‚úÖ Understand District-specific expectations
- ‚úÖ Build relationships with inspectors
- ‚úÖ Identify potential issues early
- ‚úÖ Streamline approval process
- ‚úÖ Reduce risk of non-compliance

### 2.2 When to Engage

**Recommended Engagement Points:**

1. **Early Planning Phase** (12-18 months before submission)
   - Initial introduction and relationship building
   - General questions about requirements
   - Understanding District-specific processes

2. **During Plan Development** (6-12 months before submission)
   - Specific technical questions
   - Clarification on ambiguous requirements
   - Review of draft sections (if District allows)

3. **Pre-Submission** (1-3 months before submission)
   - Final questions and clarifications
   - Submission process confirmation
   - Timeline expectations

4. **Post-Submission** (after submission)
   - Status inquiries (as appropriate)
   - Response to review questions
   - Clarification requests

### 2.3 How to Engage

**Initial Contact:**
- Identify appropriate District Office contact
- Use formal communication channels
- Be professional and prepared
- Have specific questions ready

**Communication Methods:**
- **Email**: Primary method for non-urgent questions
- **Phone**: For urgent matters or complex discussions
- **In-Person Meetings**: For significant issues or relationship building
- **Formal Letters**: For official requests or submissions

**Best Practices:**
- Be concise and specific in questions
- Provide context for your questions
- Reference specific regulations or guidance
- Follow up appropriately
- Document all communications

### 2.4 Questions to Ask

**General Questions:**
- What is the typical review timeline for Cybersecurity Plans?
- Are there District-specific requirements or expectations?
- What documentation format is preferred?
- Are there common issues to avoid?

**Technical Questions:**
- How should we interpret specific regulatory requirements?
- What level of detail is expected in the Cybersecurity Plan?
- How should we document critical asset assessments?
- What evidence is needed for compliance?

**Process Questions:**
- What is the submission process?
- How will we be notified of approval or issues?
- What is the appeals process if needed?
- How are inspections scheduled?

## 3. Cybersecurity Plan Submission Process

### 3.1 Submission Requirements

**Mandatory Deadline:**
- **July 16, 2027**: Cybersecurity Plan must be submitted to Coast Guard

**Submission Package Must Include:**
- Complete Cybersecurity Plan
- Supporting documentation
- Critical asset inventory
- Assessment results
- Any required certifications

### 3.2 Submission Process

**Step 1: Preparation**
- Complete Cybersecurity Plan development
- Gather all supporting documentation
- Review for completeness and accuracy
- Internal review and approval

**Step 2: Submission**
- Submit to appropriate District Office
- Use required submission method (check with District)
- Include all required documentation
- Maintain submission confirmation

**Step 3: Review Period**
- Coast Guard reviews submission
- May request additional information
- May request clarifications
- Review timeline varies by District

**Step 4: Approval or Conditional Approval**
- Coast Guard approves plan, or
- Issues conditional approval with requirements, or
- Requests revisions

**Step 5: Implementation**
- Implement approved plan
- Maintain compliance with plan
- Update plan as required
- Prepare for inspections

### 3.3 Submission Best Practices

**Before Submission:**
- ‚úÖ Complete all required elements
- ‚úÖ Ensure consistency across documents
- ‚úÖ Verify all references and citations
- ‚úÖ Review for regulatory compliance
- ‚úÖ Have internal experts review
- ‚úÖ Consider legal/compliance review

**During Submission:**
- ‚úÖ Follow District-specific procedures
- ‚úÖ Submit complete package
- ‚úÖ Maintain submission records
- ‚úÖ Confirm receipt with District

**After Submission:**
- ‚úÖ Monitor for requests for information
- ‚úÖ Respond promptly to questions
- ‚úÖ Maintain communication with District
- ‚úÖ Document all interactions

## 4. Inspection Preparation

### 4.1 Types of Inspections

**Routine Inspections:**
- Scheduled inspections
- Typically annual or periodic
- Comprehensive review of compliance
- May include cybersecurity focus

**Targeted Inspections:**
- Focus on specific areas
- May be triggered by incidents
- May focus on cybersecurity
- May be unannounced

**Follow-Up Inspections:**
- Verify corrective actions
- Check implementation of requirements
- May be scheduled or unannounced

### 4.2 Pre-Inspection Preparation

**Documentation Organization:**
- Organize all required documentation
- Ensure easy access to records
- Prepare summary documents
- Have key personnel available

**Facility Preparation:**
- Ensure physical security is current
- Verify cybersecurity measures are implemented
- Check that training records are current
- Confirm drills/exercises are documented

**Personnel Preparation:**
- Brief key personnel on inspection process
- Ensure CySO is available
- Have subject matter experts ready
- Prepare for questions

### 4.3 Documentation to Have Ready

**Required Documentation:**
- Cybersecurity Plan (approved version)
- Critical asset inventory
- Cybersecurity Assessment results
- Training records
- Drill/exercise documentation
- Incident reports (if any)
- Recordkeeping documentation
- Technical cybersecurity measures documentation

**Supporting Documentation:**
- Policies and procedures
- Network diagrams
- Access control documentation
- Monitoring and detection logs
- Vendor contracts and assessments
- Change management records

## 5. What to Expect During Inspections

### 5.1 Inspection Process

**Typical Inspection Flow:**

1. **Opening Meeting**
   - Inspector introduction
   - Inspection scope and objectives
   - Facility overview
   - Schedule for inspection

2. **Documentation Review**
   - Review of Cybersecurity Plan
   - Review of assessments
   - Review of training records
   - Review of drill/exercise documentation
   - Review of incident reports

3. **Facility Walk-Through**
   - Physical security review
   - Cybersecurity measures verification
   - System and network review
   - Access control verification

4. **Interviews**
   - CySO interview
   - IT/OT personnel interviews
   - Security personnel interviews
   - Management interviews

5. **Closing Meeting**
   - Preliminary findings discussion
   - Questions and clarifications
   - Next steps
   - Timeline for formal report

### 5.2 Inspector Focus Areas

**Cybersecurity Plan Compliance:**
- Plan completeness and accuracy

**Critical Assets:**

**Drills and Exercises:**
- Drill/exercise frequency

**Technical Measures:**
- Implementation of controls

**Incident Response:**

### 5.3 Common Inspection Activities

## 8. Response Strategies for Findings

### 8.1 Types of Findings

**Minor Findings:**
- Documentation gaps
- Process improvements needed
- Administrative issues
- Typically non-enforcement

**Major Findings:**
- Significant compliance gaps
- Missing required elements
- Implementation failures
- May result in enforcement

**Critical Findings:**
- Serious non-compliance
- Safety or security risks
- Immediate action required
- Likely enforcement action

### 8.2 Response Process

**Step 1: Understand Finding**
- Review finding carefully
- Understand requirement
- Clarify with inspector if needed
- Assess severity

**Step 2: Develop Corrective Action Plan**
- Identify root cause
- Develop corrective actions
- Assign responsibilities
- Set timelines

**Step 3: Implement Corrective Actions**
- Execute corrective actions
- Document implementation
- Verify effectiveness
- Update documentation

**Step 4: Report to Coast Guard**
- Submit corrective action plan
- Provide implementation updates
- Request verification inspection if needed
- Maintain communication

### 8.3 Best Practices for Response

**Immediate Actions:**
- ‚úÖ Acknowledge finding promptly
- ‚úÖ Take immediate action if safety/security risk
- ‚úÖ Communicate with Coast Guard
- ‚úÖ Document all actions

**Corrective Action Development:**
- ‚úÖ Address root cause, not symptoms
- ‚úÖ Develop comprehensive plan
- ‚úÖ Set realistic timelines
- ‚úÖ Assign clear responsibilities

**Implementation:**
- ‚úÖ Execute plan thoroughly
- ‚úÖ Document all actions
- ‚úÖ Verify effectiveness
- ‚úÖ Update documentation

**Communication:**
