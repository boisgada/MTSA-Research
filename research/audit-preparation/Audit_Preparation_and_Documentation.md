# Audit Preparation and Documentation Guide for MTSA Compliance

## Purpose

This guide provides comprehensive guidance on preparing for Coast Guard inspections and audits, organizing documentation, collecting evidence, and maintaining audit trails. It ensures organizations are ready for Coast Guard review and can demonstrate compliance effectively.

## 1. Audit Preparation Overview

### 1.1 Types of Audits and Inspections

**Routine Inspections:**
- Scheduled Coast Guard inspections
- Comprehensive compliance review
- Typically annual or periodic
- May include cybersecurity focus

**Targeted Inspections:**
- Focus on specific areas
- May be triggered by incidents
- May focus on cybersecurity
- May be unannounced

**Plan Review:**
- Cybersecurity Plan submission review
- Documentation review
- Approval process
- May request additional information

**Follow-Up Inspections:**
- Verify corrective actions
- Check implementation
- May be scheduled or unannounced

### 1.2 Audit Preparation Objectives

**Preparation Goals:**
- Demonstrate compliance effectively
- Provide complete documentation
- Answer questions accurately
- Show continuous improvement
- Maintain professional relationship

**Preparation Benefits:**
- Smooth inspection process
- Positive inspection outcomes
- Reduced findings
- Faster approval processes
- Stronger relationships

## 7. Pre-Audit Checklist

### 7.1 Documentation Checklist

- [ ] Cybersecurity Plan current and complete
- [ ] All supporting documentation available
- [ ] Critical asset inventory complete
- [ ] Assessments current and complete
- [ ] Training records complete
- [ ] Drill/exercise documentation complete
- [ ] Incident reports documented (if any)
- [ ] Technical documentation current
- [ ] Records organized and accessible
- [ ] Version control maintained

### 7.2 Implementation Checklist

- [ ] All plan requirements implemented
- [ ] Technical controls operational
- [ ] Training completed
- [ ] Assessments completed
- [ ] Drills/exercises conducted
- [ ] Incident response ready
